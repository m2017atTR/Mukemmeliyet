# Mukemmeliyet
Mükemmel Dökümantasyonlar



I've been messing around with artificial intelligence for a few days now. Is it perfect? Yes...

But in short, after a few minutes of trial, this was my conclusion:

It’s like an apprentice applying for a job who has tinkered with a few documents and knows some concepts, but has never been able to fully complete any task.  
When you ask questions, their approach is correct on every topic, but they can’t give an in-depth answer.

These job interviews have always been exhausting.

That’s what AI is like.

Soon, just like in the movie *Her*, we’re going to start seeing people addicted to AI. Let’s not forget how the YouTuber craze began.  
Or social media influencers and how they evolved.  
We’ll soon be facing the problems that come with it — a search for perfection that isn’t actually all that perfect (at least, as of today*1).

The conclusion: The companies that control the taps have entered an extremely expensive(*1.1) process to market their own products and increase product diversification, and just like the cloud hype, they seem ready to start swallowing people up.

*1 For any mediocre AI, there’s a need for terabytes of RAM. Where is all that investment and production coming from? :)  
*2 Are the costs of this stream being covered using resources already waiting for us to buy them in the cloud (remember the previous cloud gate case)? As a result, even if I never use it, is there an indirect cost for me? Of course there is…

Our far-from-perfect artificial (synthetic, fake, phony, bogus) intelligence craze — let’s call it *Fake-Intelligence Gate* — well, good luck with that.
